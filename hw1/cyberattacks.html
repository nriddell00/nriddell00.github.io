What is a Cyber Attack?

A cyber attack is an attempt deigned to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system, network, or device. Cyber attacks can take many forms, including malware, phishing, ransomware, and denial of service.

Types of Cyber Attacks:

  1. DoS (Denial of Service):

    DoS attacks are designed to make a network or system unavailable to its intended users by overwhelming it with traffic or other requests.

  2. MITM (Man-in-the-Middle):

    MITM attacks occur when an attacker intercepts and potentially alters communication between two parties who believe they are communicating directly with each other.

  3. Phishing:

    Phishing is a type of cyber attack that uses email or other forms of communication to trick individuals into providing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card numbers.

  4. Ransomware:

    Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment in exchange for the decryption key.
